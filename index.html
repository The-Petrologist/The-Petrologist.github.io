Internships

Wadia Institute of Himalayan Geology (WIHG), Dehradun.

Project: Petrographic and Raman Spectroscopic Study of Fluid Inclusions in Metamorphic Regime

This study investigates fluid inclusions preserved within garnetiferous mica-schist associated with Barrovian-type regional metamorphism. Fluid inclusions serve as micro-scale records of metamorphic processes, preserving information on fluid composition, entrapment conditions, and post-entrapment modifications.

Petrographic analysis was employed to document inclusion morphology, distribution, and phase assemblages in host minerals such as quartz, zircon, feldspar, and muscovite. The inclusions, observed along primary growth zones and intragranular trails, were classified as monophase, biphase, or complex morphologies. Raman microspectroscopy was utilized to characterize the chemical composition of the inclusions and confirm host mineral identities.

The combined petrographic and spectroscopic data suggest that the inclusions were trapped under medium-grade metamorphic conditions, consistent with the biotite‚Äìgarnet zone of Barrovian metamorphism. The presence of aqueous-rich, two-phase inclusions and textural indicators such as poikiloblastic growth and schistosity reflect a dynamic metamorphic fluid history involving both progressive metamorphism and deformation-related re-equilibration.
