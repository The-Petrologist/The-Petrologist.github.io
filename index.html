Academic Field Experience

3rd Year Field Trip – Regional Geology

As part of the 3rd year Curriculum at IISER Bhopal, we Conducted a comprehensive geological field trip across parts of Madhya Pradesh, exploring diverse lithologies including Deccan Traps and Vindhyan Sandstones. The fieldwork provided hands-on experience in using instruments such as the clinometer and Brunton compass, as well as in stereonet projection for structural data analysis. Observed and documented features like spheroidal weathering, quartz veins, dikes at Potla, conglomerates, and river terraces.

Studied sedimentary structures including ripple marks, herringbone structures, load casts, and cross-bedding. Conducted geological mapping of the Sanchi Stupa area using fore-bearing and back-bearing (triangulation) techniques.

Location: Madhya Pradesh, India

Duration: February 10 – 14, 2025

Internships

Wadia Institute of Himalayan Geology (WIHG), Dehradun.

Project: Petrographic and Raman Spectroscopic Study of Fluid Inclusions in Metamorphic Regime

This internship focused on the investigation of fluid inclusions preserved within garnetiferous mica-schist belonging to a Barrovian-type regional metamorphic belt. Fluid inclusions act as micro-scale geochemical archives, providing valuable insights into the composition, entrapment conditions, and evolution of metamorphic fluids.

Detailed petrographic analysis was carried out to examine inclusion morphology, phase assemblages, and spatial distribution within minerals such as quartz, feldspar, zircon, and muscovite. The inclusions were documented along primary growth zones, healed microfractures, and intragranular trails, and were classified into monophase, biphase, and multiphase types.

Raman microspectroscopy was employed to determine the molecular composition of the inclusions and confirm the mineralogical identity of host minerals. This technique helped distinguish CO₂-bearing and aqueous-rich inclusions and provided evidence of fluid–rock interaction during metamorphism.

Integrated petrographic and Raman data indicate that the inclusions were trapped under medium-grade metamorphic conditions, consistent with the biotite–garnet zone of Barrovian metamorphism. The presence of two-phase aqueous inclusions, inclusion trails, and poikiloblastic textures suggests a dynamic fluid history influenced by progressive metamorphism and deformation-related re-equilibration.

Internship under the supervision of: Dr. Barun Kumar Mukherjee, Scientist “E”, Petrology and Geochemistry Group, Wadia Institute of Himalayan Geology, Dehradun.

Duration: May 5 – June 28, 2025
